The experiment you are going to participate aims at studying the decision process. We ask you to read the full instructions. These instructions should let you understand the experiment.
The experiment is composed of two parts. The instructions below are those of part 1. The screen will display the instructions for part 2 when the first part will be over.
During the experiment the payoff are in ecus. The conversion rate of ecus in euros is given in instructions of each part.

 

Part 1

At the beginning of the experiment the server will form random groups of two participants. You are not able to identify the other player in your group and he/she is not able to identify yourself. You are endowed with 20 tokens. You must decide how you divide these 20 tokens between two accounts: your individual account and a collective account that is common to the two members of your group (you and the other member). You concretely have to enter on the screen the number of tokens you put in the collective account. The computer will automatically put the rest of the tokens in your individual account. You are free to put any tokens you want in the collective account. The only constraint is that it has to be an integer comprise between 0 and your endowment, that is 0 token, 1 token, ..., 19 tokens, 20 tokens (your full endowment).

 

The accounts

Your individual account

Each token placed in your individual account earns you 1 ecu. Your individual account only earns ecus for you alone. So, for example, if you put 6 tokens on your individual account, this will earn you 6 ecus.

The collective account

The collective account is shared by you and the other member of your group. Each token placed in the collective account earns 0.6 ecus to each member of the group (you and the other member of the group). Similarly, each token placed in the collective account by the other member of your group earns you 0.6 ecus. Thus, if for example, you place 6 tokens in the collective account, this will earn 3.6 ecus for each member of the group (you included).
If for example there are 30 tokens in the collective account, your payoff from this account is 30 x 0.6 = 18 ecus. The payoff from this account of the other member of your group will also be 18 ecus.

 

Gain

Your gain is equal to the sum of the gains from your individual account and the collective account.

 

Example

You place 14 tokens in the collective account and thus 6 tokens in your individual account. Let us suppose that the other member of your group places 12 tokens in the collective account. In total, the collective account now comprises 14+12 = 26 tokens. Your gain is then equal toÂ  6 ecus (individual account) + 26 x 0.6 (collective account) = 21.6 ecus.

 

Final details

You will have only one decision to take.
You and the other participants to this experiment will make your decision independently from each other. After all decisions are made and the experiment completed, the computer will randomly create groups of two participants, which will allow to compute each one's payoffs according to the description above. Within one week after the completion of the experiment, you will receive a feedback about your decision and the decision made by the other member of your group.
